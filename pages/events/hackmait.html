HackMAIT 4.0

Hackathon

As part of its annual technical fest, Impulse, IOSD (MAIT) is organizing a 26-hour in-person hackathon with a variety of themes ranging from Ed-Tech, to Healthcare, and Cybersecurity. With HackMAIT 4.0, our aim is to foster innovation and creativity captured in the young minds of college students, all the while encouraging a focus on learning, having fun, and making friends.

The event will consist of 3 rounds:

  • Round 1(Ideation Round): The preliminary stage is conducted online, wherein participants must present their ideas and provide a detailed project PPT submission.
  • Round 2 (Qualifying Round): Participants are expected to work on their projects and present them to the judges/mentors during the on-campus round from 9 am to 5 pm. It is not necessary to fully implement the complete idea during this round.
  • Round 3 (Presentation Round): In the final round, each team is required to demonstrate their functional project in front of the pannel.

Rules

Registration Guidelines

  • Registration of teams must be done by all the members of the team.
  • Teams can register under any single track mentioned in the brochure/rulebook, however only one registration per team is allowed.

Participation Guidelines

  • All work on a project should be done during the hackathon. But the teams can plan their basic layout before the hackathon. The github repository will be created during the hackathon.
  • During the 26-hour Hackathon, participants will be required to remain on campus until 5 pm. Following this, they can leave and return the next day at 9 am.
  • Teams can use any libraries, frameworks, or open-source code in their projects. Working on a project before the event and open-sourcing it for the sole purpose of using the code during the event is against the spirit of the rules and is not allowed.
  • Teams must stop hacking once the time is up. However, teams are allowed to debug and make small fixes to their programs after time is up. e.g. If during demoing your hack you find a bug that breaks your application and the fix is only a few lines of code, it's okay to fix that. Making large changes or adding new features is not allowed.
  • Teams can be disqualified from the competition at the organizers' discretion.
  • Mentors will be provided during the hackathon for solving the doubts of participants.
  • No team member is allowed to register under more than one team.
  • Outside Delhi participants can show their presence online. But participants from Delhi are required to be present at the campus.

General Rules

  • Inter-college teams are allowed
  • Each team would mandatorily comprise 2-4 members (at most).
  • It's mandatory for each participant to carry a College ID card for the hackathon.
  • All decisions in matters of eligibility, authenticity & marking made by the judges shall be final and binding.
  • Any attempts at malpractice and/or plagiarism will lead to disqualification.
  • All the event-specific rules and guidelines will be conveyed before the event.
  • Organizers reserve the right to change, modify or postpone any aspect of the event without prior notice. In case of any dispute, the decision of the organizers shall be final and binding
  • Teams failing to adhere to the deadlines will be disqualified
  • All the participants have to reach the venue 15 min before the time.
  • Each round will be an eliminatory round

What is a Hackathon?

A Hackathon is an intense event that brings together people like software developers, graphic designers and user interface specialists along with industry process experts and professionals to identify issues and create software solutions.

What do I need to bring to the event?

This is a very hands-on event. Bring your laptops and mobile phones, but above all, your energy and your brain!

Can students from other colleges participate?

YES. Anyone can participate but the event will be in offline mode and online for outside Delhi-NCR students

1. BlockChain & Cybersecurity

  • Problem Statement (Alternatives): Centralized systems are often vulnerable to cyber attacks and data breaches, as they provide a single point of failure for hackers to target. How might we use blockchain technology to create decentralized and secure systems that can replace traditional centralized systems and enhance cybersecurity?
    Challenge Description: Participants in this hackathon are challenged to develop blockchain-based solutions that can replace traditional centralized systems and enhance cybersecurity by providing decentralized and secure alternatives.
  • Problem Statement (Defi): The process of auditing and validating transactions on traditional financial systems can be time-consuming and prone to error, making them vulnerable to fraud and cyber attacks. How might we use blockchain technology to create more secure and efficient financial systems that are resistant to fraud and cyber attacks?
    Challenge Description: Participants in this hackathon are challenged to develop blockchain-based solutions that can create more secure and efficient financial systems that are resistant to fraud and cyber attacks. Potential solutions could include blockchain-based smart contracts that automate and secure financial transactions, decentralized finance (DeFi) platforms that enable secure and transparent peer-to-peer financial transactions, or blockchain-based identity management solutions for secure authentication and authorization of financial transactions.

2. Smart Education

  • Problem Statement (EdTech): With the rapid digitization of education, there is a need for smarter and more personalized approaches to learning that can cater to the diverse needs of students. How might we leverage emerging technologies to create smart education solutions that can provide personalized learning experiences, foster collaboration and engagement, and promote skill development?
    Challenge Description: Participants in this hackathon are challenged to develop smart education solutions that can provide personalized learning experiences, foster collaboration and engagement, and promote skill development.
    Potential solutions could include personalized learning platforms that leverage artificial intelligence and machine learning to adapt to individual student needs and preferences, virtual and augmented reality platforms that provide immersive and interactive learning experiences, or gamification platforms that can promote engagement and skill development.
  • Problem Statement (Culture & Language): With the increasing demand for global citizenship education, there is a need for smarter tools and platforms that can help students to develop intercultural competence, empathy, and understanding. How might we leverage emerging technologies to create smart education solutions that can promote global citizenship education and foster cultural understanding and empathy?
    Challenge Description: Participants in this hackathon are challenged to develop smart education solutions that can promote global citizenship education and foster cultural understanding and empathy.
    Potential solutions could include virtual exchange platforms that facilitate cross-cultural interactions and dialogue between students from different countries and backgrounds, AI-powered language learning tools that can provide personalized feedback and support for language learning, or gamification platforms that can promote engagement and motivation in global citizenship education.

3. Social Impact

  • Problem Statement (Inequality): In today's society, many people are facing social issues such as poverty, inequality, and discrimination. How might we use technology to create solutions that can address these issues and promote social impact?
    Challenge Description: Participants in this hackathon are challenged to develop technology solutions that can help solve social issues and promote social impact. Solutions can be aimed at helping marginalized communities, promoting diversity and inclusion, or addressing specific social issues such as poverty, inequality, or discrimination.
    Potential solutions could include platforms or applications that facilitate communication and collaboration among community members, technologies that help individuals connect with resources and support services, or tools that help organizations better track and measure their social impact.
  • Problem Statement (Climate): Climate change is an urgent global challenge that requires innovative solutions to reduce greenhouse gas emissions and promote sustainability. How might we use technology to create solutions that can reduce our environmental impact and promote sustainability?
    Challenge Description: Participants in this hackathon are challenged to develop technology solutions that can help reduce greenhouse gas emissions, improve resource efficiency, and promote sustainability in different areas such as transportation, agriculture, or energy.
    Potential solutions could include platforms or applications that promote sustainable behavior and lifestyle choices, technologies that enable better monitoring and management of natural resources, or tools that encourage the adoption of renewable energy sources.

How might we use technology to create solutions that can improve access to basic healthcare services and promote better health outcomes?
    Challenge Description: Participants in this hackathon are challenged to develop technology solutions that can help improve access to basic healthcare services, reduce healthcare costs, and promote better health outcomes for individuals in need.

How might we leverage emerging technologies to create smart fitness solutions that can provide high-quality virtual training and support to people in remote or underserved areas?

How might we use technology to create innovative solutions that can help remote teams work together more effectively, build strong team relationships, and improve team performance?
    Challenge Description: Participants in this hackathon are challenged to develop innovative solutions that can help remote teams work together more effectively, build strong team relationships, and improve team performance.
    Potential solutions could include AI-powered tools that help managers track and manage team performance, virtual team-building exercises that promote collaboration and communication, or digital platforms that connect remote workers and provide opportunities for social interaction and networking.
